Typography: Serif vs Sans-serif

This is a Serif Font

Serif fonts have small lines or strokes regularly attached to the end of a larger stroke in a letter or symbol within a particular font. They are often used in traditional print publications.

This is a Sans-serif Font

Sans-serif fonts lack the small lines or strokes at the end of characters. They are considered modern and are commonly used in digital content due to their clear and straightforward appearance.
